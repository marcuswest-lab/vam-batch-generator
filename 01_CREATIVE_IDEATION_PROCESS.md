# VAM Creative Ideation Process
*Weekly repeatable process for generating creative batches. Follow these steps each cycle.*

---

## Weekly Overview

| Day | Activity | Output | Time |
|-----|----------|--------|------|
| Monday | Performance Review + Hook Generation | Updated reference files + 20-30 new hooks | ~1 hr |
| Tuesday | Static Batch 1 | 5 static ad briefs (proven winner variations) | ~30 min |
| Wednesday | Video Batch 1 | 5 video ad briefs (UGC cost/savings angle) | ~30 min |
| Thursday | Static Batch 2 | 5 static ad briefs (adjacent/experimental hooks) | ~30 min |
| Friday | Video Batch 2 OR Copy Batch | 5 video or copy-only briefs | ~30 min |

**Weekly output: 15-20 new creatives across 3-4 batches**

---

## Step 1: Performance Review (Monday — 15 min)

### What to do:
1. Export the latest data from the VAM Creative & Copy Tracking Sheet (all 3 tabs: Static, Video, Copy)
2. Categorize every active ad into one of four buckets:

| Bucket | Criteria | Action |
|--------|----------|--------|
| **Scale** | ROAS above 1.5x OR cost per connected below $350 | Increase budget. Do NOT create variations yet — let it run. |
| **Optimize** | ROAS 1.0-1.5x OR cost per connected $350-$500 | Create 2-3 variations (new hook on same visual, or same hook on different visual) |
| **Test** | Less than $500 spend | Let it run to $500+ before judging |
| **Kill** | ROAS below 0.8x with $500+ spend, OR CPL above $80 with 20+ leads | Turn off. Document in 04_LOSING_PATTERNS.md |

3. Update these reference files:
   - `04_WINNING_HOOKS_LIBRARY.md` — Add any new winners, promote emerging hooks
   - `04_LOSING_PATTERNS.md` — Add any new confirmed losers
   - `04_PERFORMANCE_SNAPSHOT.md` — Update current KPIs and top performers
   - `02_MASTER_SYSTEM_PROMPT.md` — Update the "Current highest numbers" section

### Key questions to answer:
- What are the top 5 performing hooks right now?
- What are the top 3 performing visuals right now?
- Are any "On the fence" ads ready to be promoted to Winner or killed?
- What new formats are showing early promise?

## Step 2: Hook Generation via 70-20-10 (Monday — 30 min)

### Generate 20-30 hooks using this breakdown:

### 70% Proven Winners (14-21 hooks)
Pull from `04_WINNING_HOOKS_LIBRARY.md`. For each winning hook category, generate 3-4 new variations by rotating through the 8 Verbal Hook Types:

**Current winning categories to mine:**

| Category | Example Winners | New Hook via Different Verbal Type |
|----------|----------------|-----------------------------------|
| Social Proof Shock | "Check this", "Dodged 4k bill" | Question: "Did you know most movers charge 2x what you should pay?" |
| Price Anchoring | "Starting at $1,597" | Label: "Long-distance movers — your cross-country move might cost less than rent" |
| Cost Exposure | "Almost paid double", "How movers sneak extra fees" | Conditional: "If you're getting quotes from movers, you need to see this first" |
| Savings | "Save up to 50%" | Command: "Stop overpaying for your move" |
| Emotional Relief | "Stress free", "Moving checklist" | Narrative: "I was dreading this move. Then I found out about..." |
| Humor | "Flying funny" | Exclamation: "This can't be real... $1,597 to move across the country?!" |

**8 Verbal Hook Types rotation:**
1. Labels — "Long-distance movers:"
2. Questions — "Did you know...?" / "Would you pay...?"
3. Conditionals — "If you're moving cross-country..."
4. Commands — "Stop overpaying" / "Read this before you book"
5. Statements — "The smartest thing you can do before moving..."
6. Lists/Steps — "3 things movers don't want you to know"
7. Narratives — "I almost paid $4,000 for my move. Here's what happened..."
8. Exclamations — "This is insane..." / "I can't believe this is real"

### 20% Adjacent Winners (4-6 hooks)
Model hooks from other service industries. Industries to mine:
- **Home services** (roofing, HVAC, plumbing) — "You're overpaying" dynamics
- **Insurance comparison** — "I switched and saved $X" dynamics
- **Car dealerships** — Price anchoring, limited-time pricing
- **Travel deals** — "I found flights for $X" surprise/share dynamics
- **Real estate** — "Before you buy/rent" advice positioning

### 10% Experimental (2-3 hooks)
Try completely untested angles. Current opportunities:
- Fear-of-loss: "What happens when the movers drop your grandmother's china?"
- Time-savings: "Get 3 weeks of your life back"
- Contrarian: "Why the cheapest mover will cost you the most"
- Social media native: reaction-style, stitch-style, green screen over review
- Specificity play: "Moving a 3-bedroom from Texas to Oregon for $2,100"

## Step 3: Awareness Level Distribution (Monday — 10 min)

### Map each hook to an awareness level:

| Level | Hook Approach | % of Weekly Output | VAM Example |
|-------|--------------|-------------------|-------------|
| Most Aware | Offer-driven — lead with price/deal | 30% | "Long-distance moves starting at $1,597 — get a free quote" |
| Product Aware | Proof-driven — lead with social proof | 25% | "See why 12,000 families chose VAM for their cross-country move" |
| Solution Aware | Promise-driven — lead with outcome | 20% | "Move your entire home cross-country in 14 days or less" |
| Problem Aware | Pain-driven — lead with the pain point | 15% | "Tired of getting ghosted by movers after they give you a quote?" |
| Unaware | Curiosity-driven — lead with intrigue | 10% | "The hidden fee buried in 90% of moving quotes that adds $2,000" |

**Why this matters:** VAM's current ads are heavily skewed toward Most Aware (offer/price hooks). The gap to 2x ROAS is in reaching colder audiences through Problem Aware and Solution Aware hooks.

### Check your distribution:
- If more than 40% of hooks are "Most Aware" → push some toward Product/Solution Aware
- If 0% are "Unaware" → add at least 1-2 curiosity hooks per week

## Step 4: Kaleidoscope Assembly (Monday — 20 min)

### Combine hooks × meat types × CTAs into batches of 5

**Available Meat Types for VAM:**

| Meat Type | Static Format | Video Format | VAM Performance |
|-----------|--------------|-------------|-----------------|
| Testimonial | Gmail screenshot, Twitter-style, Apple Note | UGC direct-to-camera | BEST |
| Faceless | Gmail, Apple Note, text-on-truck-photo | Text overlay on footage | STRONG |
| Demonstration | Before/after, crew working, loaded truck | Timelapse, crew footage | MODERATE |
| Story | Customer journey series | Narrative UGC, mini-doc | EMERGING |
| Education | DO NOT USE | DO NOT USE | LOSER |

**CTAs (pick 1-2 per batch):**
- "Get your free quote in 60 seconds"
- "See what your move actually costs"
- "Compare your moving costs — free"

### Batch Assembly Structure:
Each batch of 5 should follow this slot structure:

| Slot | Purpose | Hook Source | Awareness Level |
|------|---------|-------------|-----------------|
| 1 | **Anchor** | Top proven winner (reuse or close variation) | Most Aware |
| 2 | **Variation** | Slot 1 hook in different visual OR different hook on Slot 1 visual | Product Aware |
| 3 | **Expansion** | Proven winner adapted for broader audience | Solution Aware |
| 4 | **Adjacent** | Hook modeled from another niche | Problem Aware |
| 5 | **Wild Card** | 10% experimental | Problem Aware or Unaware |

## Step 5: Generate Briefs (Tuesday-Friday — 30 min per batch)

### For each batch:

1. Open a new Claude session
2. Paste the contents of `02_MASTER_SYSTEM_PROMPT.md` as context
3. Paste the appropriate batch generator:
   - **Static batch:** Use `02_BATCH_GENERATOR_STATIC.md`
   - **Video batch:** Use `02_BATCH_GENERATOR_VIDEO.md`
   - **Copy batch:** Use `02_BATCH_GENERATOR_COPY.md`
4. Fill in the Batch Parameters section with your batch focus
5. Reference the hooks you generated in Step 2
6. Review the output against the Post-Generation Checklist
7. Make any edits needed

### Suggested weekly batch schedule:

**Tuesday — Static Batch 1 (Proven Winner Variations)**
- Focus: Variations of your top 2-3 static winners
- Visual priority: SV8_Gmail + SV11_TwitterStyleLight
- Awareness: Heavy on Most Aware + Product Aware
- Example prompt: "Generate a batch focused on social proof Gmail variations, building on SC7_CheckThis and SC9_AlmostPaidDouble"

**Wednesday — Video Batch 1 (UGC Cost/Savings)**
- Focus: UGC testimonials with cost/savings hooks
- Talent: Asian woman (proven 8-11% CTR) or new demographic test
- Awareness: Mix across Most Aware to Problem Aware
- Example prompt: "Generate a UGC batch with cost angle hooks, targeting Asian woman talent, building on VHK20 and VHK22"

**Thursday — Static Batch 2 (Adjacent/Experimental)**
- Focus: New hook angles on proven visuals
- Visual priority: Mix including SV6_TruckFreeway (re-emerging) + SV31_AppleNoteLight
- Awareness: Push into Solution Aware + Problem Aware
- Example prompt: "Generate an experimental static batch testing Problem Aware hooks on proven visual styles"

**Friday — Video Batch 2 OR Copy Batch**
- Focus: Either new UGC demographic OR copy-only tests
- For copy: Test new hooks against proven body copy (CB1/CB3)
- Example prompt: "Generate a copy batch testing new hook types against CB3_CostSavings body"

---

## Step 6: Submit to ClickUp (After each batch)

1. Copy the completed batch brief output
2. Open the Creative Capacity Sheet to estimate due date
3. Submit via the ClickUp Creative Request Form — one form per batch
4. Fill in the Priority/Timely identifiers
5. Assign to:
   - **Static batches** → Lead Designer
   - **Video batches** → Lead Video Editor
   - **Copy batches** → tag in the static or video batch request
6. Include the batch category/label in the submission

---

## Step 7: Weekly Close (Friday — 5 min)

1. Confirm all batches were submitted to ClickUp
2. Note which hooks/angles you're most excited about testing
3. Flag any hooks that need UGC talent sourcing for next week's video batches
4. Update `04_PERFORMANCE_SNAPSHOT.md` if new data came in mid-week

## Scaling the System

As you get comfortable with the process:

| Stage | Batches/Week | Creatives/Week | Timeframe |
|-------|-------------|----------------|-----------|
| **Starting** | 2-3 | 10-15 | Weeks 1-2 |
| **Steady State** | 3-4 | 15-20 | Weeks 3-8 |
| **Scaled** | 5-6 | 25-30 | Week 8+ |

To scale beyond 4 batches/week:
- Run the batch generators multiple times per day with different focus parameters
- Add a Wednesday copy-only batch to test hooks faster
- Test new visual styles (SV36+) with proven hooks
- Expand UGC talent roster for more demographic testing
